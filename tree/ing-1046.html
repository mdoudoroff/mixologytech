    • brandies

    description

    Brandy is a generic category for liquor produced from the fermented juice and/or solids of fruit. In cocktail recipes, the unqualified specification of “brandy” almost always implies an aged grape brandy such as Cognac. If some other kind of brandy is intended, there will usually be additional context (e.g., “apple brandy” or “Spanish brandy”).

    Note that in the United States, an unfortunate confusion of nomenclature persists where certain liqueurs (sweet) and eau de vies (bone dry, unaged, true fruit brandies) share names. For example, in cocktail recipes, “apricot brandy” usually indicates a sweet, apricot-flavored liqueur—not a true brandy at all. Meanwhile, true apricot brandy can also be found on the shelves. Caveat emptor.
