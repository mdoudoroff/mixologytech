    lime wedge

    • garnishes & solids /
    • fruit, fresh /
    • citrus fruit /
    • lime /
    • lime wedge

    description

    A lime wedge is a wedge-shaped cut of a fresh lime that typically corresponds to one sixth of the fruit. To cut lime wedges, trim off the tips (poles) of the lime. Bisect the lime from pole-to-pole. Lay one lime half flat and slice two times from pole-to-pole, at 60-degrees and 120-degrees to yield three even pieces.

    flavors
    sour, citrus, fruit, lime
    substitute
    other fresh seasonal fruit (imperfect substitute).
