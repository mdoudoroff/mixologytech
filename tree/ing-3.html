    mezcal

    description

    Mescal is a generic category for Mexican spirit produced in whole or in part from agave succulents, some very cheap, others produced using strictly traditional methods in tiny quantities. Tequila is the best-known variety of mescal and enjoys legal protections. Mescal that is not labeled “Tequila” is of a different variety.

    In common practice, “mescal” is used to imply any agave spirit that isn’t Tequila and to imply a rougher, smokier character, although neither distinction is strictly accurate. Mescal encompasses a relatively broad range of styles and traditions, of which Tequila is one carefully regulated regional example.
