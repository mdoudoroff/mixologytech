Bacardi

  • brands
    • Bacardi

description

Famous rum brand, as well as the third largest liquor company in the world. Bacardi began in Santiago, Cuba in the 19th Century, grew immensely throughout the 20th Century and expanding operations to Puerto Rico, Mexico, Spain and elsewhere. Bacardi lost its Cuban operations as a result of the Cuban Revolution; since then, Bacardi has redefined itself as a global company rather than a Cuban one. Bacardi operates the largest distillery in the world in Puerto Rico, as well as other distilleries elsewhere.

Recipes predating the 1960’s or so that specify Bacardi do not actually have in mind the product that currently bears the brand. Assuming you don't have access to a stash of 1950’s Bacardi, you have to improvise. Most likely, you’re looking for an aged white or gold molasses-based rum that is light in body, with pleasing rum character, some complexity, but with no specific dominating flavors and not too much raw alcohol/vodka in the nose or throat. The closest substitute is probably Havana Club 3 Year, which is the national Cuban brand (embargoed in the United States, but widely available elsewhere), but you also might consider the light bodied rums from Puerto Rico, the Dominican Republic, St. Croix (“Santa Cruz”), and the Central American and Venezuelan distillers.

Recipes from the 1970s on that specify Bacardi are generally referring to the current Bacardi products.
