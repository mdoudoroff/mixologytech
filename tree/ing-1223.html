    • Bacardi, Carta de Oro

    synonyms

    Bacardi Gold Seal rum

    description

    Defunct gold Cuban rum. The less-filtered expression of Bacardi's signature rum originating in Santiago, Cuba. During the first half the 20th Century, Bacardi expanded production to Puerto Rico, Mexico and Spain in order to meet growing demand, while generally keeping a tight lid on quality control. Vintage bottles may state the country of origin. During the decades following the Cuban Revolution, Bacardi reinvented itself along a globalized model that drastically changed their products and marketing. No current Bacardi product really resembles the original Carta de Oro, although Bacardi Gold is ostensibly its descendent.

    Cuban rum is rum from Cuba, but it specifically implies the light-bodied style of rum as established and epitomized by the Bacardi rums prior to the Cuban Revolution. The emphasis of this style is distilling to high degree of purity (employing continuous stills). All Cuban rum of this style is aged, although some is stripped of its color (and some of its flavor) through filtering, while others are amber from age and the judicious addition of caramel color for consistency.
