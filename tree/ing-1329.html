    curaçao, white

    • liqueurs /
    • liqueurs, fruit-flavored /
    • orange-flavored liqueur /

    • curaçao, white

    description

    White curaçao is generic for un-colored (distilled) curaçao, but note that the “white curaçao” found in recipes circa 1900-1940 are most likely references to triple sec. The distinction is somewhat hazy, although white curaçao will often be lower proof with a milder orange flavor than triple sec.

    flavors
    orange, citrus, fruit, alcohol, sweet
    substitute
    Triple sec, other orange-flavored liqueur, or home-made. To make a simple curaçao, infuse fresh and/or dried orange peels in pure grain alcohol (for a period of days or weeks) then dilute and sweeten to suit taste and purpose. (Recipes abound.)
