    Cointreau

    • Cointreau

    description

    Proprietary, highly-refined French neutral-spirit-based curaçao orange liqueur that defined the triple sec category. The company, in fact, coined “triple sec” to describe their product, but later dropped the term from their label when hosts of imitators deluged the market with lower quality generic products in similar bottles and with the term "triple sec" in a font like that of Cointreau.

    Cointreau is an indispensable cocktail component.

    Triple sec is a branch of curaçao and a generic category for neutral spirit-based refined colorless orange-flavored liqueurs imitative of Cointreau. (Cointreau itself was originally labeled “Triple Sec Curaçao” before trademark concerns led the owners to focus entirely on their brand name.) Some 19th century bar guides appear to use the term “white curacao” to refer to triple sec.

    The principle distinction of triple sec relative to other curaçaos is that the base is a neutral conveyance for a very clean, sophisticated orange flavor, unattainable before the invention of the continuous still. Cheap triple secs usually fall short on sophisticatation.

    Commentary

    Based on a study of several thousand recipes from the 19th and 20th Century, Cointreau is the second-most commonly specified brand in the history of cocktail recipes by a hefty margin, but a distant second to Angostura Bitters.
