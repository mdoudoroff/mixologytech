    synonyms

    orange curaçao

    description

    Curaçao is a generic category for a simple orange liqueur, either distilled or infused ("home made"). Commercial bottlings are typically available in various colors but with similar flavor to one another, in neutral spirit base. Any specified color, such as "Blue Curaçao", always denotes the addition of food coloring for visual effect. Without food coloring, distilled curaçao is naturally colorless; infused curaçao usually has some orange/yellow tinting from the citrus peels used during infusion.

    Note that triple sec is a term for distilled curaçao that began when distilleries such as Cointreau set out to produce a more refined liqueur. The “white curaçao” references found in recipes circa 1900-1940 can be presumed to mean triple sec.

    flavors

    orange, citrus, fruity

    substitute

    Triple sec, other orange-flavored liqueur, or home-made. To make a simple curaçao, infuse fresh and/or dried orange peels in pure grain alcohol (for a period of days or weeks) then dilute and sweeten to suit taste and purpose. (Recipes abound.)
