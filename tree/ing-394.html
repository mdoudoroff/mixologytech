    Okolehao

    • liqueurs /
    • liqueurs, spirit-flavored /
    • Okolehao

    synonyms

    oke

    description

    Proprietary branding for what was once a generic Hawaiian folk spirit, traditionally a distillate of ti root, which produces a similar quantity and quality of sugar syrup to the better-known sugar cane. “Okolehao” translates literally from the Hawaiian as "iron bottom," a reference to to style of cooking pot from which the liquor was distilled.

    Defunct in recent decades, with some sporadically-available dubious interpretations that are essentially an American whiskey liqueur sweetened by ti sugar extract. As of 2012, at least one legitimate revival product may now be available in some markets from Haleakala Distillers.

    Spirit-flavored liqueurs is an abstract category for sweetened and flavored spirits of various dilutions featuring the flavor of a particular spirit.
