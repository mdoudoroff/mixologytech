    cinnamon

    • garnishes & solids /
    • herbs & spices /
    • cinnamon

    synonyms

    powdered cinnamon

    description

    Cinnamon is an aromatic spice derived from the dried inner bark of any of several varieties of tropical evergreen tree used as a flavoring additive for drinks in extract from or as a constituent of liqueurs and bitters—and as a garnish either as a tightly rolled rod (cinnamon stick) or as a ground powder sprinkled over the surface of a drink. Most cinnamon on the market is actually cassia, which is extensively cultivated in Asia, but also was known Classically in Arabia. True cinnamon is almost all from Sri Lanka. As spices they are distinct, but interchangeable for most drink-related purposes.
