    Kina Lillet DEFUNCT

    • fermented & brewed alcoholic ingredients /
    • wine, grape /
    • wine, fortified /
    • aperitif wine (fortified) /
    • quinquina /
    • Kina Lillet

    description

    Legendary, proprietary bitter quinine aperitif wine from Bordeaux.

    Considerable confusion hangs over this product, which originated in the late 19th Century and remained fairly obscure in most markets throughout the 20th Century. Inconsistent labeling contributed to the confusion, as product labeled as just “Lillet” or even “Lilet” appeared in some markets, with no mention of “kina”. As of 2012—officially—these were all the same product regardless of label, which lives on today as “Lillet Blanc” (joined by Rouge and Rose expressions). If so, then Kina Lillet was a profoundly mild quinquina and this entry is redundant with that for “Lillet Blanc”.

    Some believe or suspect that “Kina Lillet” was originally significantly more bitter than today’s Lillet Blanc, and either reformulated or evolved to its present character in the late 20th Century. If so, no cocktail calling for significant quantities of “Kina Lillet” can be accurately reproduced today.

    Quinquina is generic for sweetened fortified aperitif wines containing quinine (thus “quinquina”) and spices. The major brands are: Bonal, Byrrh, Dubonnet, Lillet, and St. Raphael. The quinine is the bitter constituent which promotes production of digestive enzymes and, therefore, appetite. Most quinquinas are produced from mistelle (fortified grape juice) rather than actual fermented wine.

    Brand: Lillet
