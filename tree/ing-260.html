    gin, orange-flavored

    • neutral spirits & grain eau de vie /
    • gin /
    • gin, flavored /
    • gin, orange-flavored

    description

    Nominally a dry gin with natural or artificial orange flavoring and little or no sweetening.

    Caveat emptor: recipes in British bar guides during American Prohibition and thereafter calling for “orange flavored gin” often are indicating an orange liqueur with a gin base. And example is/was produced domestically by Charles Jacquin et Cie of Philadelphia, Pennsylvania.

    flavors
    alcohol, juniper, orange, citrus, herbal, fruit, floral, spice
    substitute
    if the ingredient is intended to sweeten a drink, substitute an orange liqueur such as triple sec; otherwise, muddle a few orange twists in the dry gin of your choice
    places (country/region of production)
    United States, Canada, United Kingdom, England (United Kingdom)

    examples

    • Beefeater Orange
    • Bloomsbury Orange Flavoured Gin
    • Seagram’s Orange Twisted Gin
