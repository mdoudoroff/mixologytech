    Marie Brizard Orange Curaçao

    • Marie Brizard Orange Curaçao

    description

    Marie Brizard Orange Curaçao is a proprietary example of curaçao.

    Curaçao is a generic category for orange liqueur, distilled and/or infused ("home made"). Origins are Dutch, but became ubiquitous in the 19th Century.

    Contemporary commercial bottlings are typically simple, inexpensive orange liqueurs available in various colors but with similar flavor to one another, in neutral (or mostly-neutral) spirit base. Any specified color, such as "Blue Curaçao", always denotes the addition of food coloring for visual effect. Without food coloring, distilled curaçao is naturally colorless; infused curaçao usually has some orange/yellow tinting from the citrus peels used during infusion.

    Curaçao evolved two branches beginning in the late 19th Century: one from brandy producers, who employed their aging stocks and attention to detail to produce high end curaçao exemplified by Grand Marnier. The other branch was triple sec, a term for column-distilled curaçao that began when distilleries such as Cointreau set out to produce a highly refined liqueur built on neutral spirits. The “white curaçao” references found in recipes circa 1900-1940 can usually be presumed to mean triple sec.

    Brand: Marie Brizard

    flavors
    orange, citrus, fruit, alcohol, sweet
    substitute
    Triple sec, other orange-flavored liqueur, or home-made. To make a simple curaçao, infuse fresh and/or dried orange peels in pure grain alcohol (for a period of days or weeks) then dilute and sweeten to suit taste and purpose. (Recipes abound.)
