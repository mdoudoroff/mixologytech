    Smirnoff

    • brands
      • Smirnoff

    description

    The history of the Smirnoff brand is convoluted, but in 1938, after a long sequence of developments, the license to produce and sell vodka in the United States under the Smirnoff brand fell the hands of the Heublein concern of Connecticut. After some mis-steps, Heublein succeeded in creating a vast market for their product in the United States (initially by marketing the product as “white whiskey”). The initial popular success of Smirnoff in the U.S.A. indirectly led to the world-wide popularity of vodka today. As of this writing, Smirnoff is owned by Diageo and is one of the top-selling distilled spirit brands in the world. The brand itself originated in Russia and moved several times, but today is principally American. The provenance of what’s actually in a bottle of Smirnoff is hazy and may vary by product and market.
