    lemon wedge

    • garnishes & solids /
    • fruit, fresh /
    • citrus fruit /
    • lemon /
    • lemon wedge

    description

    A lemon wedge is a wedge-shaped cut of a fresh lemon that typically corresponds to one eighth of the fruit. To cut lemon wedges, trim off the tips (poles) of the lemon. Bisect the lemon from pole-to-pole. Lay one lemon half flat and slice three times from pole-to-pole, at 45-degrees, 90-degrees and 135-degrees to yield four even pieces.

    flavors
    citrus, sour, lemon, fruit
    substitute
    other citrus fruit

    examples

    • lemon wedge dipped in sugar
    • lemon wedge dipped in sugar and fresh coffee grounds
    • lemon wedge studded with cloves
