    • Lemon Hart 151-proof Demerara Rum

    description

    Proprietary, flavorful, high-proof dark Demerara rum. This particular product has proven uniquely indispensable as an ingredient in many “exotic” tropical drinks to the extent that it is its own category. As of this writing, this rum has no meaningful competition in its de facto category, although competition would be welcomed. While not a distinguished rum on its own from a connoisseur’s perspective, the combination of flavor and proof offer something essential that cannot be otherwise reproduced.

    151-proof Demerara rum is generic for overproof rum produced in Guyana in both white (filtered) and dark (caramel-colored) styles.

    Commentary

    This product was held by Pernod Ricard for many years until it was sold off in February 2010 to French Canadian concern Mosaic. A disruption of distribution ensued. The product is back in some markets with new labeling; in the United States, its availability is due to Herculean efforts by importer Caribbean Spirits Inc. As always, the rum itself continues to be produced under contract by Demerara Distillers Ltd.

    Lemon Hart 151 remains a unique and essential rum for various purposes, but especially for exotic drinks. A particularly good showcase for the spirit is the Don the Beachcomber’s “151 Swizzle”: 1/2 oz fresh lime juice, 1/2 oz simple syrup, 1 1/2 oz Lemon Hart 151, 1 dash Angostura bitters, 6 drops Pernod and 8 oz crushed ice. Blend for 5 seconds and pour into a glass. Dust with nutmeg and garnish with a cinnamon stick. (Adapted from Beachbum Berry’s Grog Log, SLG Publishing, 1998).
