“Will there be an Android version?”

No. That is the short answer.

The long answer is long, and doubtless incomplete, but here goes:

App development is expensive. To produce the underlying framework for these products—mainly in terms of programming—and the products themselves required a cash outlay on the order of a very nice imported automobile. Factoring in other development costs, we’re in supercar territory. That’s just to get these products out for iOS.

Cocktail apps are niche products. They will never sell in the millions, and even hundreds of thousands is stretch. That means that recouping the initial investment at $10 a sale takes a long while.

Working against us is the extreme downward price pressure of mobile apps. If these apps were selling at the same rate, but everybody was paying $40 each instead of $10, not only would the entire business proposition be more reasonable, but the apps would have more features and could be developed more aggressively. But if I charged $40 each for these apps, sales would probably crater because $40 is perceived as too much to pay for apps, even by highly motivated customers. That’s the world we live in.

To create and maintain Android versions of these apps would be like investing in another very nice imported automobile long before the first one is paid off. Factor in that app revenues from Android are typically around 25% those of iOS (Android customers buy far fewer apps than iOS customers) and the prospects look remote. So, never say never, but for now, it’s actually far more realistic for sufficiently-motivated Android users to pick up an iPad to run these apps than it is for me to provide them with Android versions. I wish it wasn’t so.

—Martin
